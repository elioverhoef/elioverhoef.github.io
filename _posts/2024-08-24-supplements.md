# My 91 Weekly Supplements for Optimal Health and Longevity

Hi, I'm Elio, and today I'm going to share my personal supplement stack - 91 pills per week, or 14 different supplements taken daily or as needed. This might seem like a substantial amount, but each supplement serves a specific purpose in my quest for optimal health, energy, and longevity.

Before we dive in, a brief disclaimer: I've conducted extensive research on these supplements, adjusted my intake based on personal reactions, and undergone blood tests to inform my choices. This approach is tailored to my specific needs and may not be suitable for everyone. Always consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new supplement regimen.

## Why I Take Supplements

My supplement regimen is designed to achieve three primary goals:

1. Living in optimal health
2. Maintaining peak energy levels
3. Extending my lifespan

While a balanced diet forms the foundation of good health, supplements play a crucial role in my health strategy. They're not a replacement for nutritious food, but rather an addition to ensure I'm covering all aspects of my nutritional needs.

## Detailed Breakdown of My Supplement Stack

### 1. Omega-3

**Dosage**: 3 pills (1g EPA total)

Omega-3 fatty acids are crucial for brain and heart health. They also play a significant role in reducing inflammation throughout the body. I take a high-quality fish oil supplement to ensure I'm getting enough EPA (eicosapentaenoic acid), which is particularly beneficial for cardiovascular health.

**Consideration**: If you consume fatty fish like salmon, mackerel, or sardines regularly, you might not need an Omega-3 supplement.

### 2. Vitamin D

**Dosage**: 4000IU (reduced to 2000IU in summer)

Vitamin D is essential for bone health, immune function, and mood regulation. Many people are deficient in this crucial vitamin, especially those who don't get much sun exposure. I adjust my dosage seasonally, taking less in the summer when I'm naturally getting more vitamin D from sunlight.

**Consideration**: If you spend a lot of time outdoors in sunny conditions, you might not need as high a dose.

### 3. Pure Brainpower

**Dosage**: 100mg coffee fruit extract

This supplement contains coffee fruit extract, which has been shown to boost levels of Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor (BDNF). BDNF is crucial for cognitive function, learning, and memory.

**Consideration**: If you're sensitive to caffeine, you might want to avoid this supplement or take it earlier in the day.

### 4. L-Tyrosine

**Dosage**: 500mg

L-Tyrosine is an amino acid that serves as a precursor to dopamine and other neurotransmitters. It can help improve mood and cognitive function, especially under stressful conditions.

**Consideration**: If you don't face significant stress in your daily life, you might not need this supplement.

### 5. L-taurine

**Dosage**: 500mg

Taurine is an amino acid that supports nervous system function and heart health. It's also known for its potential to improve exercise performance and reduce fatigue.

**Consideration**: If your diet is rich in meat and seafood, you might already be getting sufficient taurine.

### 6. Boron

**Dosage**: 3mg

Boron is a trace mineral that plays a role in bone health and testosterone regulation. It's not as well-known as other minerals, but research suggests it could be important for overall health and hormonal balance.

**Consideration**: If you don't have concerns about bone health or hormonal balance, you might not need to supplement with boron.

### 7. Ashwagandha KSM-66

**Dosage**: 1.5g of extract

Ashwagandha is an adaptogenic herb that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries. It's known for its ability to reduce stress and anxiety, and may also have cognitive-enhancing properties.

**Consideration**: If you don't experience high levels of stress or anxiety, you might not need this supplement.

### 8. NMN (Nicotinamide Mononucleotide)

**Dosage**: 500mg

NMN is a precursor to NAD+, a coenzyme that plays a crucial role in cellular energy production and DNA repair. Some research suggests it may have anti-aging effects, though more studies are needed.

**Consideration**: This is a relatively new supplement, and long-term effects are still being studied. It's primarily of interest if you're focused on anti-aging strategies.

### 9. Creatine

**Dosage**: 5g

Creatine is one of the most well-researched supplements available. It's known for improving exercise performance and muscle growth, but it may also have cognitive benefits.

**Consideration**: If you're not engaged in regular, intense physical activity, you might not need to supplement with creatine.

### 10. NAC (N-Acetyl Cysteine)

**Dosage**: 700mg

NAC is a precursor to glutathione, one of the body's most powerful antioxidants. It's known for its potential to support liver health and may also have mood-stabilizing properties.

**Consideration**: If you don't have specific concerns about oxidative stress or liver health, you might not need this supplement.

### 11. Apigenin

**Dosage**: 50mg

Apigenin is a flavonoid found in many plants, particularly in chamomile tea. It's known for its antioxidant properties and may help reduce anxiety and improve sleep quality.

**Consideration**: If you regularly consume chamomile tea, you might already be getting a good amount of apigenin.

### 12. Magnesium L-threonate

**Dosage**: 400mg

This form of magnesium is thought to be particularly good at crossing the blood-brain barrier. It may help improve cognitive function and memory.

**Consideration**: If you don't have specific cognitive concerns, other forms of magnesium might be sufficient.

### 13. L-theanine

**Dosage**: 200mg

L-theanine is an amino acid found in tea leaves. It's known for promoting relaxation without causing drowsiness and may help improve focus and sleep quality.

**Consideration**: If you're a regular tea drinker, especially green tea, you might already be getting a good amount of L-theanine.

### 14. Alpha-GPC

**Dosage**: 300-600mg (not taken daily)

Alpha-GPC (alpha-glycerophosphocholine) is a choline-containing compound that easily crosses the blood-brain barrier. It's a precursor to acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter involved in memory, attention, and muscle contraction.

**Benefits**:

- May improve cognitive symptoms in older adults with mild to moderate dementia
- Potentially enhances athletic performance, particularly in power output and vertical jump power
- Could improve the effectiveness of treatments for certain neurological conditions

**Considerations**:

- Generally well-tolerated, but recent studies suggest a potential link to increased cardiovascular risk, especially in those already at high risk for CVD
- More research is needed to confirm long-term effects and safety
- If you have a history of cardiovascular issues, consult with a healthcare provider before use

## Supplement Timing

Timing can play a crucial role in maximizing the benefits of certain supplements. Here's how I structure my supplement intake throughout the day:

### Morning

I take the majority of my supplements in the morning. This includes Omega-3 (2 pills), Vitamin D, L-Tyrosine, L-taurine, Boron, Ashwagandha KSM-66, NMN, Creatine, and NAC.

### Afternoon

In the afternoon, I take:

- Pure Brainpower
- Alpha-GPC (300mg or 600mg, not daily, for focus)
- 1 pill of Omega-3

### Evening

I take my sleep stack in the evening, which includes:

- Apigenin
- Magnesium L-threonate
- L-theanine

This timing strategy allows me to leverage the energy-boosting and cognitive-enhancing effects of certain supplements during the day, while promoting relaxation and better sleep quality in the evening.

Remember, the timing that works best for me might not be optimal for everyone. It's important to listen to your body and adjust your supplement schedule based on your individual needs and lifestyle.
